What is a waste exemption?

If your business uses, treats, recovers, stores or disposes of waste, you may need an environmental permit.

However if your waste operation is below specific limits, it may be exempt from needing a permit and can operate under a waste exemption instead.

Waste exemption rules and limits

Each exemption has strict limits and conditions. You must check the waste exemption guidance to make sure you can follow them.

If your waste operation does not meet the limits and conditions of an exemption you will need an environmental permit.

If you do not follow the rules, your waste operation is not exempt and the Environment Agency will ‘de-register’ your exemption. If you do not meet the limits and conditions of an exemption, or your waste operation has harmed people or the environment, you may be prosecuted.

Exemption costs and renewal

Waste exemptions are free except for the T11 WEEE exemption which costs £1,221.

An exemption registration lasts 3 years. You’ll need to renew the exemption after 3 years if you are still using it.

Choose the right exemptions

Storing waste

These cover the storage of certain waste materials in secure containers and at secure sites, and storing sludge.

Using waste

These cover operations such as using waste in construction, spreading certain materials on the land, and burning of waste as fuel.

Disposing of waste

These cover operations such as depositing sanitary waste, depositing agricultural waste and spoil from dredging inland waters, and burning specific waste in an incinerator or in the open.

Treating waste

These cover operations such as preparing, recovering, sorting and treating certain waste materials, such as waste wood and plant matter.

Register or renew waste exemptions

When you have found the right exemption, and are satisfied that you can meet the limits and conditions, you need to register it with us.

Your waste exemption registration will need to be renewed every 3 years, if you are still using it.

Before you start

Before you start, you will need:

  • The code numbers of the exemptions you want to register (for example, U1 - use of waste in construction).
  • The name, address and contact details of the person, business or organisation carrying out the waste operation.
  • The site address and postcode where the exempt activity is being carried out, or a 12-digit National Grid reference if there is no postcode.
  • For a limited company or limited liability partnership, the company registration number and registered address.
Start now

T11 WEEE exemption

The T11 exemption to treat waste electrical and electronic equipment (WEEE) cannot be registered online. To register a T11 WEEE exemption, complete form WEEEX001 and send it to the Environment Agency address on the form. It costs £1,221 to register this exemption.

Bulk registrations and linear networks

Road, river, canal or railway - linear networks

If you want to register U1, U12, D1 or D2 exemptions on a stretch of road, river, canal or railway (a linear network) you should complete the linear network registration form.

The current online registration system only works for sites that have a single defined place.
