When you need a waste exemption

A waste exemption is a waste operation that is exempt from needing an environmental permit. Each exemption has limits and conditions that you must comply with.

To see if there is an exemption for your waste operation, and check that you meet the conditions, see choose the right exemptions.

If you cannot meet the limits and conditions of an exemption, you must apply for an environmental permit.

Costs

Waste exemptions are free to register, and registration lasts for 3 years.

The exception is the T11 WEEE exemption, which covers repairing or refurbishing waste electrical and electronic equipment (WEEE). This costs £1,221 to register for 3 years. To register this exemption, use form WEEEX001.

Choose the right exemptions

Waste exemptions are listed in 4 categories. Read the guidance to:

  • decide which waste exemptions apply to your operations
  • check you can meet all the limits and conditions

Using waste (exemptions U1 to U16)
These cover operations such as using waste in construction, spreading waste materials on the land and burning of waste as fuel.

Disposing of waste (exemptions D1 to D8)
These cover operations such as depositing sanitary waste, depositing agricultural waste and spoil from dredging inland waters, and burning specific waste in an incinerator or in the open.

Storing waste (exemptions S1 to S3)
These cover the storage of certain waste materials in secure containers and at secure sites, and storing sludge.

Treating waste (exemptions T1 to T33)
These cover operations such as preparing, recovering, sorting and treating certain waste materials, such as waste wood and plant matter.

Register or renew your exemptions

When you have found the right exemption, and checked that your operation meets the limits and conditions, you should register it with us.

Your waste exemption registration will need to be renewed every 3 years, if you are still using it.

Before you start

You'll need to know:

  • the exemptions you want to register (for example, U1 use of waste in construction)
  • the name, address and contact details of the person, business or organisation carrying out the waste operation
  • the site address and postcode where the exemption will be used, or a National Grid reference (for example, ST 58132 72695)
  • for a limited company or limited liability partnership, the company registration number and registered address

If you do not follow the rules, your waste operation will not be exempt and the Environment Agency will ‘de-register’ your exemption.

Non waste framework directive exemptions

You do not need to register the 3 non waste framework directive exemptionscovering the temporary storage of waste, but you must follow the limits and conditions they set out.

Bulk registrations and linear networks

Bulk registrations

If you have more than 30 sites you can register using the bulk and linear networks registration form.

Linear networks – road, river, canal or railway

If you want to register U1, U12, D1 or D2 exemptions on a stretch of road, river, canal or railway (a linear network), you should complete the linear network registration form.

The current online registration system only works for sites that have a single defined place.
