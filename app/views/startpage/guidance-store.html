Storing waste: waste exemptions

Read through the list of exemptions for storing waste. The storing waste exemptions cover the storage of specific waste materials in secure containers and at secure sites, and storing sludge. You need to comply with the limits and conditions set out.


Published 1 February 2016
Last updated 6 April 2018
From: Department for Environment, Food & Rural Affairs and Environment Agency


A waste exemption is a waste operation that is exempt from needing environmental permit. Each exemption has specific limits and conditions you need to operate within.

The exemptions for storing waste cover a range of operations, such as the storage of specific waste materials in secure containers and at secure sites, and storing sludge to be used in accordance with the Sludge (Use in Agriculture) Regulations 1989.

If you want to operate under a waste exemption you must register that exemption with the Environment Agency. If you donâ€™t, you will not be exempt from needing a permit.

You register T22 with the authority responsible for granting an authorisation under the Animal By-products Regulations.

Registering an exemption does not remove the need to apply for other permits or permissions. For example, you may need planning permission for your waste operation, or a water discharge permit.
