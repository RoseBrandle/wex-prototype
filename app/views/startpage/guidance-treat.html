Treating waste: waste exemptions

Read through the list of exemptions for treating waste. The treating waste exemptions cover operations such as preparing, recovering, sorting and treating specific waste materials. You need to comply with the limits and conditions set out.


x

Published 1 February 2016
Last updated 6 April 2018
From: Department for Environment, Food & Rural Affairs and Environment Agency


A waste exemption is a waste operation that is exempt from needing environmental permit. Each exemption has specific limits and conditions you need to operate within.

The exemptions for treating waste cover a range of operations, such as treating, sorting, repairing, refurbishing or dismantling waste for transport, reuse or recovery. These include treating organic materials to produce fertiliser and biogas, cleaning waste packaging and textiles for recycling or transportation, and treating oil filters, fluorescent tubes and toner cartridges before recovery.

There are 3 waste treatment exemptions that you need to register with other authorities (not the Environment Agency). These are:

  • T3 – treatment of waste metals and metal alloys by heating for the purposes of removing grease
  • T7 – treatment of waste bricks, tiles and concrete by crushing, grinding or reducing in size
  • T22 – treatment of animal by-product waste at a collection centre

You register T3 and T7 with the local authority where you have your principle place of business. If this is outside England and Wales, it should be with the local authority where the operation will be carried out.

You register T22 with the authority responsible for granting an authorisation under the Animal By-products Regulations.

A full description of T3, T7 and T22 can be found at Environmental Permitting (England and Wales) Regulations 2010.
